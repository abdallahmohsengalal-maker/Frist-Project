Project 1

The students responsible for preparing the project, namely: Mohamed Khaled Radwan, Mahmoud Tolba, Sherif Ashraf, Abdelrahman Hesham, Nora Mohamed, Mohamed Hani, Hani Shaker, Ibrahim Said, and Ibrahim Rashed, stated that the importance of the project lies in developing electrical substations in general, improving their control systems, and reducing the number of personnel required at the station, which in turn lowers operational costs.

Project 2

The team also indicated that the substation is operated automatically without the need for on-site personnel, as the entire station is monitored and supervised by a control system that can be managed either locally through a control unit inside the station or remotely via a control center. This is achieved by adding a practical component that simulates the remote control system.

Project 3

At the beginning, the team clarified that the project involves designing a power substation for a private compound to step down the voltage from 220 kV to 22 kV to supply distribution feeders, and then feeding distribution transformers that reduce the voltage from 22 kV to 380 V for residential use.
